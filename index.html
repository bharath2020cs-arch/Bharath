About

Design that holds up on a shelf, a screen or a wall.

I'm Mukesh. I design brand identities, posters, packaging and the occasional app interface — mostly for small studios, indie labels and food brands that want to look like themselves and nobody else.

Most projects start on paper. I sketch thumbnails before anything opens on a screen, then move into Illustrator or Figma once a direction earns its place. I like work that ends up printed, packaged or pinned to a wall just as much as work that lives behind glass.

How a project runs

01 — Brief

Brief & research

A short call to understand the brand, the audience and what the design needs to do — followed by reference gathering and quick thumbnails.

02 — Direction

Concept & design

Two or three directions, built out properly rather than as rough sketches, so the choice is based on something real.

03 — Delivery

Refine & deliver

One direction refined to final, with print-ready files, source files and a short usage guide handed over at the end.
